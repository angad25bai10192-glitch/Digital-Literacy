# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where scammers trick users into transferring money through deceptive tactics. A typical scenario involves a fraudster contacting a victim via phone call, SMS, or messaging apps, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or buyer/seller.

Step 1: The attacker gains the victim’s trust by creating urgency or offering a deal.  
Step 2: They ask the victim to scan a QR code or click a malicious link.  
Step 3: The victim is instructed to “receive money” but actually ends up authorizing a payment.  
Step 4: The victim unknowingly enters their UPI PIN, completing the transaction.  
Step 5: The money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account.

College students and new digital users are often targeted because they may not fully understand how UPI transactions work. The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and in some cases, difficulty recovering the stolen money.

This type of fraud spreads quickly due to the convenience and speed of digital payments. Awareness and caution are essential to prevent such incidents.
